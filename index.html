About Me

I am an MS student in Agricultural Extension and Rural Development at Gazipur Agricultural University, with an academic background in Fisheries. My work is grounded in field-based learning, institutional analysis, and practical exposure to extension systems.

Through internships and field activities, I have worked closely with government fisheries offices, fish farmers, hatchery operators, feed mills, fish markets, and rural communities. These experiences shaped my interest in how policies, extension services, and local institutions translate into real outcomes at the farmer level.

I am particularly interested in fisheries extension, rural livelihood diversification, technology adoption, training systems, and evidence-based planning. I value data not as an abstract tool, but as a way to improve communication, service delivery, and development impact.
